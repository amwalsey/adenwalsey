Turbine Blade Design

Designed and tested turbine blades for Cornell’s wind tunnel to maximize power output while maintaining structural integrity under high rotational speeds.

Objective

Optimize blade geometry to achieve peak efficiency near 1500 RPM while staying within material limits.

Approach

Developed a MATLAB aerodynamic model using TSR = 6, iterated pitch and taper, and validated structural stresses using ANSYS simulations.

Results

Achieved improved predicted power output while maintaining stresses below PLA failure limits.
